Part 1: The Crown（王室）

King's legal status, immunity, and the Crown Proceedings Act 1947

1.1 国王的法律地位

The King is the legal head of state（法定国家元首），but by convention（惯例），the prime minister and government act in the King's name.

国王的法定角色

  • Appointing the PM — 由能获得下议院多数支持的政党领袖出任
  • Appointing ministers — PM提名，King正式任命
  • Summoning / Proroguing / Dissolving Parliament — 现由PM决定（Fixed-Term Parliaments Act 2011，每5年大选，但下议院2/3多数可提前大选，或政府不信任投票且无替代政府时提前大选）
  • Royal Assent — 惯例上从不拒绝，纯属形式
  • Granting honours — 政府提名，King授予
Bagehot's trinity（三项权利）: The right to advise, encourage, or warn the PM or minister. 国王可表达意见，但最终由大臣决定。

1.2 Crown Proceedings（王室诉讼）

King's personal immunity

The King can do no wrong. 不承担刑事责任、民事责任，不可被逮捕。此豁免不延伸至王室其他成员。

Crown Proceedings Act 1947 Key Provisions
保留刑事豁免 Crown仍不受刑事起诉
s.1 政府可在合同中被诉（sued in contract）
s.2 政府在侵权中承担责任，包括 vicarious liability（代位责任）
s.21(1) 禁止对 Crown 发出 injunction（禁制令）和 specific performance（强制令）
s.40(2) Crown 不受议会法案约束，除非法案明文规定
Armed forces 现役军人不可起诉，但 armed forces (Crown Proceedings) Act 1987 暂停此豁免（战时可恢复）
M v Home Office [1994] — 法院将"Crown"解释为女王个人。法院可对政府大臣发出禁制令，大臣如不遵守可被控 contempt of court（藐视法庭）。
Factortame (No 2) [1991] — 法院可对国务大臣发出 interim injunction（临时禁制令）。
Act of State（国家行为原则）: UK government actions taken against foreign citizens outside the UK are not actionable. Crown Proceedings Act 1947 未改变此原则。
内政部即将驱逐一名被认定为非法移民的人。其律师能否阻止？

可以。如果被驱逐者有合法抗辩理由应留在英国，法院可发出 injunction 命令内政部不得驱逐。（M v Home Office）

1.3 Summary: The Crown

What is the Crown?

Either the King personally or His Majesty's government.

  • King has many legal powers, used by the government after consultation
  • King personally cannot be sued, but the government can be sued

Part 2: The Royal Prerogative（王室特权）

Definition, remaining powers, and judicial identification
Royal Prerogative（王室特权）: The remaining legal powers of the King, exercised by the government in his name. 属于 executive powers（行政权）— 法律上政府不需议会许可即可使用。甚至可不经议会同意制定 Orders in Council（枢密院令）。

2.1 仍然存在的特权权力

CategoryExamples
Government & Civil Service控制文官系统，设立新机构（BBC由royal charter设立）
Justice System维持王国治安，任命 King's Counsel
National Security情报与安全服务
Foreign Affairs与他国关系，签署和批准国际条约
Defence of the Realm控制武装部队，部署军队

2.2 法院如何判断哪些特权仍然存在？

政府承认无法编制确定性的特权清单，争议由法院裁决。

BBC v Johns [1965] — BBC声称作为Crown的一部分可免税 → 法院：无历史先例 → 不存在此特权

De Keyser's Royal Hotel [1920] — 政府战时征用财产的特权存在，但须支付补偿

ex parte Northumbria Police [1987] — 法院确认政府始终有维持王国治安的特权

2.3 特权权力的范围

R (Miller) v The Prime Minister [2019] UKSC 41（脱欧prorogation案）

首相Johnson决定中止议会34天。最高法院裁定：不存在合法的中止议会如此长时间的特权权力。
议会拥有主权，必须被允许立法和追究政府责任。行政机关不能阻止议会。
通常能判断是否涉及特权 — 如果问题涉及 King 或 PM 的行为，很可能就是特权权力。

2.4 Summary: Royal Prerogative

Key Facts

  • What is it? The remaining legal powers of the King
  • Who decides which remain? The courts
  • Who uses it? The government acting in the King's name

Part 3: Prerogative and Acts of Parliament（特权与议会法）

Statute supersedes prerogative · Treaties · Revival
Core Rule: Statute supersedes prerogative（制定法优于特权）
If the government has the same power under the prerogative and statute, it must use the statutory power. Parliament's sovereignty must be respected.
De Keyser's Royal Hotel [1920]
政府战时征用酒店。法院裁定：因已有 Defence of the Realm Act 1914，政府须依据该制定法补偿 → 制定法提供更好的公民保护。
R v Secretary of State, ex parte Fire Brigades Union [1995]
刑事伤害补偿方案最初由特权创立，后被 Criminal Justice Act 1988 取代，但大臣从未实施该法。大臣提议用特权创建补偿更低的方案。
上议院裁定：行政机关不能用特权挫败议会意志。废除立法是议会的权力。

→ 如果制定法被废除，特权权力可恢复（revive）。

3.1 Treaties and Statute

R (Miller) v Secretary of State for Exiting the EU [2017] UKSC 5
签署国际条约是特权权力，但条约本身不改变英国国内法。只有议会通过立法才能将条约内容纳入国内法。

3.2 Coexistence

政府通常拥有混合的制定法和特权权力。

Example: UK公民身份由制定法规定，但签发护照是特权权力（授权旅行，不创造身份）。

Part 4: Judicial Review of the Prerogative（特权的司法审查）

GCHQ Case framework · Justiciable vs non-justiciable
Council of Civil Service Unions v Minister for the Civil Service [1985] AC 374（GCHQ Case）

Lord Diplock 确立：
① 法院可以审查某些特权（是否存在该权力、权力范围、行使方式）
② 但有些特权是 non-justiciable（不可司法审查）

4.1 Non-Justiciable Prerogatives

涉及高度政治性的政府决策 → 法院拒绝审查：

  • Defence（国防）：核武器、海外部署
  • Foreign policy（外交政策）：如加入EU条约

4.2 Justiciable Prerogatives

涉及个人权利而非政府政策 → 可被审查：

  • Refusal of a passport（拒发护照）
  • Refusal of a royal pardon（拒绝赦免）
  • Government assistance to UK nationals abroad（海外公民协助 — 除非完全不理性或严重违反人权）
R (Bancoult) No 2 v Secretary of State [2016] UKSC 35
英国驱逐查戈斯群岛居民为美军基地让路。看似严重侵犯人权，但政府以国防/安全为由 → 最高法院最终支持政府（政策优先于个人权利）。
政府行使特权 → 是否涉及个人权利？ ├── 是 → 可能可审查（如拒发护照） └── 否（国防/外交等高政策）→ 不可审查（non-justiciable）
Jane被外国政府无审判羁押。家属要求英国政府交涉，政府拒绝。

法院立场： 政府什么都不做可能是不理性的（通常应协助本国公民），但法院不会具体规定政府应该做什么 — 那是外交政策，不是个人权利问题。

Part 5: Accountability to Parliament（议会对特权的问责）

Ministerial refusals to answer · Military deployment convention

法院常解释拒绝审查的理由：政府须向议会说明理由，议会是更合适的政治问责场所。

实践中大臣拒绝回答的特权事项

  • PM's advice to the King（首相对国王的建议）
  • Grant of honours（授予荣誉）
  • Commercial contracts（商业合同）
  • Prosecution decisions（检控决定）
  • Public appointments（公共任命，如法官、主教）
  • Confidential foreign discussions（与外国的保密讨论）
  • Defence & national security（国防与国家安全）

近年变化

2013年下议院拒绝同意叙利亚军事干预后，政府接受：海外部署武装力量需事先经下议院投票授权。紧急情况或人道主义干预可以是例外。

一位议员强烈反对向X国出售武器。能否阻止？

议员可在下议院提出质询或发起辩论。但政府可声称这是国防机密。政府出售武器不需议会许可。司法审查也不适用于国防等高政策领域。
问题可能问的是 convention（惯例） 而非法律。仔细审题！

Part 6: Primary Legislation — Acts of Parliament（主要立法）

Bill types · Parliamentary procedure · Royal Assent

6.1 法案类型

TypeDescription
Public Bill适用于全社会的法律，通常由政府大臣提出
Private Member's Bill也是公法案，但由普通议员（backbench MP）提出
Private Bill仅适用于特定地区/个人/机构
Hybrid Bill有普遍适用性但也影响私人利益（如HS2高铁法案）
Money Bill仅含财政措施，上议院不能阻止
Private Member's Bill ≠ Private Bill！前者是公法案（由普通议员提出），后者是适用于特定对象的法律。

6.2 议会法案程序（House of Commons）

StageDescription
Pre-legislative scrutiny政府先发布Green Paper（可能政策征询意见）→White Paper（更确定提案）→议会法律顾问撰写法律文本→内阁委员会批准。专委会进一步咨询，人权联合委员会检查人权兼容性
First reading宣读法案名称（无辩论）
Second reading辩论并投票法案原则
Committee stagePublic Bill Committee（18-25名议员）逐条审查，可修正。重要法案由全院审议
Report stage回到全院，决定是否接受委员会修正案
Third reading纠正小错误，最终批准
Fast-track procedure: 紧急法案可一日通过所有阶段。例如 EU (Future Relationship) Act 2020。

6.3 House of Lords & Parliament Acts

  • 法案须在上议院走相同阶段（上议院由全院审议委员会阶段，讨论更详细）
  • Parliament Acts 1911 & 1949: 上议院只能延迟，不能阻止普通法案（最多1年）；Money Bill最多1个月

6.4 Royal Assent & Commencement

  • Royal Assent 是形式（国王不亲自参与）
  • 大多数法案不立即生效 → 授权大臣发布命令规定生效日期
  • 不同条款可在不同时间生效
  • 部分法案可能永远不生效（如 Easter Act 1928）
Into force: An Act passed by Parliament is not the law until it is brought into force.
一项公法案在下议院和上议院走完了所有阶段，除了上议院没有三读。法案获得了御准。它是否有效？

是的。法院不会调查议会程序是否存在缺陷。议会作为主权机构，有权自行决定如何处理。

6.5 Acts of Senedd Cymru（威尔士议会法案）

  • 仅适用于威尔士
  • 四阶段：总原则 → 委员会审查 → 全院审查 → 最终表决
  • 提案主体：威尔士政府、Senedd委员会、个别议员、Senedd Commission

Part 7: Delegated Legislation（委代立法/次要立法）

Types · Parliamentary procedure · Henry VIII clauses · Judicial review
Key numbers: Parliament passes fewer than 100 Acts per year, but around 3,500 pieces of delegated legislation.
Modern Acts provide an outline/framework; the detail is filled in by delegated legislation.

7.1 Types of Delegated Legislation

TypeDescription
Statutory Instruments (SI)政府大臣制定的规则/规章/命令。引用格式：SI 1977/352（年份+编号）
Orders in Council通过枢密院制定（用于重要/宪法事项）。国王也有特权制定Orders in Council
Supreme Court rules最高法院自行制定程序规则
Bylaws地方政府/公共机构制定
Budget resolutions下议院决议变更税收（Provisional Collection of Taxes Act 1968赋予效力）

7.2 Parliamentary Procedure（议会审批程序）

ProcedureDescription
Negative Resolution自动生效，除非议会在40天内投票否决。两种形式：①制定后生效+可否决；②先呈递草案+可阻止生效
Affirmative Resolution议会必须批准。三种：①草案先批准才制定；②制定后生效前须批准；③立即生效+28/40天内不批准则失效
Super-Affirmative议会有60天审议期（如HRA 1998的Remedial Orders）
Laid（呈递）: 委代立法必须呈递至议会，告知议员。这是程序性要求。

7.3 Henry VIII Clauses

Henry VIII Clause: 议会法案中的条款，允许大臣通过委代立法修改或废除另一部议会法案的规定。
允许行政机关（而非立法机关）修改法律 → 极具争议，侵蚀议会主权。

Examples:

  • HRA 1998 s.10 — Remedial Orders（法院宣告不兼容后，大臣可修改法案）
  • European Communities Act 1972
  • EU (Withdrawal) Act 2018
Henry VIII clauses不仅用于HRA，也用于ECA 1972和EU(W)A 2018。

7.4 Judicial Review of Delegated Legislation

Acts of Parliament cannot be judicially reviewed（议会法案不可被司法审查）.
But delegated legislation CAN — the court examines whether it is ultra vires（超越母法授权范围）.
GroundCaseKey Point
Illegality Public Law Project v Lord Chancellor [2016] 母法未要求居住条件 → 大臣无权添加法律援助居住要求
Irrationality Javed v Secretary of State [2001] 将巴基斯坦指定为安全遣返国，与证据相反
Procedural Impropriety Aylesbury Mushrooms [1972] 母法要求事先咨询 → 咨询必须是真实的
Procedural Impropriety Alvi v Secretary of State [2012] 母法要求的议会审批程序未被遵守
Breach of Human Rights Ahmed v HM Treasury [2010] 枢密院令冻结资产前未给予听证（违反Art 6 ECHR）
即使委代立法已经通过议会 affirmative resolution 批准，仍可被司法审查（Javed, Public Law Project）。
Terrorism Act 2000 s.96 授权国务大臣"通过规章制定维护治安和秩序的规定"。能否据此授权无审判羁押恐怖嫌疑人？

不能。s.96的措辞必须明确授权才能如此 — 否则违法，且违反人权。

7.5 Summary: Delegated Legislation

Key Facts

  • What is it? Secondary legislation made under the authority of an Act of Parliament
  • Who uses it? Government ministers, local authorities, public bodies
  • What power? Only the power granted by the parent Act
  • If parent Act repealed → delegated legislation under it ceases to have effect

Part 8: Must-Know Exam Principles（考试必记原则）

10 core principles for SQE1 MCQs
  1. Statute supersedes prerogative — 政府有同等权力时必须用制定法（De Keyser）
  2. Treaties don't change domestic law — 只有议会立法才能将条约纳入国内法（Miller 2017）
  3. Prerogative can revive if statute repealed — 制定法废除后特权可恢复（Fire Brigades Union）
  4. Minister can't use prerogative to frustrate Parliament — 废除立法是议会的权力（Fire Brigades Union）
  5. Acts of Parliament cannot be judicially reviewed — 议会主权原则
  6. Delegated legislation CAN be judicially reviewed — ultra vires 原则
  7. Parent Act repealed → delegated legislation ceases — 母法废除则委代立法失效
  8. Parliamentary procedure defects → courts won't investigate — 法院不介入议会程序（HS2）
  9. Individual rights reviewable, high policy non-justiciable — 个人权利可审查，国防/外交不可（GCHQ）
  10. Crown can be sued in contract and tort — 但国王个人不可被诉（Crown Proceedings Act 1947）

📝 Practice Questions

Q1: Can the King be sued in court?
The King personally cannot be sued or compelled to give evidence. However, under the Crown Proceedings Act 1947, civil proceedings may be brought against the Crown (i.e. the government) in contract and tort. Crucially, no injunction can be granted against the Crown — only a declaration of illegality.
Q2: What is the relationship between prerogative powers and statutory powers?
Statute prevails over prerogative. Where Parliament has legislated on a matter, the prerogative cannot be used to override or circumvent the statute. Statute may expressly or impliedly abrogate the prerogative. However, prerogative and statutory powers can coexist where they cover different ground (Attorney General v De Keyser's Royal Hotel [1920]).
Q3: In the GCHQ case (Council of Civil Service Unions v Minister for the Civil Service [1985]), what three categories did Lord Roskill identify for judicial review of prerogative powers?
1. Justiciable — powers subject to judicial review (e.g. prerogative relating to persons, property, appointments).
2. Non-justiciable — powers not subject to judicial review (e.g. defence of the realm, foreign policy, treaties, prerogative of mercy, dissolution of Parliament).
3. Formerly non-justiciable but now justiciable — areas where the courts have expanded review (e.g. national security was reviewed in the GCHQ case itself).
Q4: A minister uses the royal prerogative to ban all trade union membership in the civil service without consultation. Is this lawful?
The use of the prerogative power itself is justiciable (per GCHQ). However, the minister failed to consult, and the courts may find the failure to consult procedurally unfair. In the actual GCHQ case, the House of Lords held the failure to consult was unlawful, but the order was upheld on national security grounds — showing that even justiciable prerogative powers can be upheld on exceptional grounds.
Q5: How is the exercise of prerogative powers held accountable by Parliament?
Three main mechanisms: (1) Parliamentary questions — MPs may question ministers about prerogative use; (2) Select committees — they scrutinise and report on government departments; (3) Ministerial responsibility — ministers must account to Parliament for their exercise of prerogative powers.
Q6: What is the difference between a Green Paper and a White Paper?
A Green Paper is a government consultation document setting out proposals for discussion and inviting public comment. A White Paper is a government policy statement, usually setting out firm legislative proposals and forming the basis for a Bill. Green Paper comes first (consultation), White Paper follows (policy commitment).
Q7: Outline the legislative process for a Public Bill to become an Act of Parliament.
1. Pre-legislative scrutiny (Green Paper → White Paper → draft Bill).
2. First Reading — formal introduction, no debate.
3. Second Reading — debate on the Bill's principle.
4. Committee Stage — detailed line-by-line examination.
5. Report Stage — further amendments considered by the whole House.
6. Third Reading — final debate on the Bill as amended.
7. The Bill passes through the other House (same stages).
8. Royal Assent — the King never refuses by convention.
9. The Act comes into force (either immediately or by commencement order).
Q8: What are the three main types of delegated legislation?
1. Statutory Instruments (SIs) — made by ministers under powers conferred by the parent Act; the most common form (≈3,000 per year).
2. Orders in Council — made by the Privy Council; can be prerogative or statutory; used for matters requiring special formality.
3. Bye-laws — made by local authorities or public bodies; only apply within the area of the making authority.
Q9: Explain the difference between the negative resolution procedure and the affirmative resolution procedure for SIs.
Negative resolution: the SI becomes law automatically unless either House passes a resolution to annul it within 40 sitting days. This is the default for most SIs.
Affirmative resolution: the SI cannot become law unless both Houses pass a resolution approving it. Used for more significant SIs where the parent Act requires it.
Key difference: negative = law unless rejected; affirmative = not law unless approved.
Q10: On what grounds can delegated legislation be challenged by judicial review?
Delegated legislation can be challenged on the ground of ultra vires (beyond the powers conferred by the parent Act):
1. Substantive ultra vires — the content of the legislation exceeds the scope of the enabling Act.
2. Procedural ultra vires — the required procedure (e.g. consultation, laying before Parliament) was not followed.
3. Unreasonableness — Wednesbury unreasonable (no reasonable authority could have made such a decision).
4. Human rights incompatibility — under HRA 1998 s 3, courts must read legislation compatibly with Convention rights if possible.
Q11: What is a Henry VIII clause and why is it controversial?
A Henry VIII clause is a provision in an Act of Parliament that allows a minister to amend or repeal the Act itself by secondary legislation, without going through the full legislative process. It is controversial because it undermines parliamentary sovereignty — the executive is effectively modifying primary law without the full scrutiny of Parliament. The courts may strike down SIs made under such clauses if they exceed the scope of the power conferred.
